Amun temple

Statue of Administrator of Amun temple, Hapi, reading from a papyrus roll. The scribe's job was one of the most important in ancient Egypt, for he was the representative of culture, science, knowledge, and literature. In this regard, scribes are considered the main founders of its civilization. The scribe's role was largely administrative, but also preserved Egypt's stories and oral traditions, just like writers today continue to fulfil the same role. The priesthood scribes in temples thus played a great part in the preservation of ancient texts through editing and revising religious, theological, ritual, medical, and magical texts. God Thoth - represented as ibis-bird - was a patron of scribes in ancient Egypt. Sandstone New Kingdom, 19th dynasty, reigns of Seti I and Ramses II Karnak temple
